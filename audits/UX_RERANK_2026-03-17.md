# Prime Self — UX Issue Re-Rank by Journey Impact

**Date:** 2026-03-17  
**Source issues:** `UX-005` through `UX-012` in `audits/issue-registry.json`  
**Rubric:** `PRODUCT_PRINCIPLES.md`

---

## Purpose

This document re-ranks the currently open UX/content items by **journey leverage**, not by when they were discovered.

The ranking is based on five questions:

1. Does the fix unblock a critical user transition?
2. Does it increase the chance the user reaches the next meaningful milestone?
3. Does it clarify the product's practitioner-first positioning?
4. Does it strengthen the "why it matters" standard?
5. Does it remove avoidable friction from a repeat-use loop?

This is a **journey-impact order**, not a pure legal/compliance order. Compliance fixes still matter, but they are tracked separately when they do not materially change progression through Journey A or Journey B.

---

## Ranked Order

### 1. UX-005 — Birth data sync from Chart to AI Profile

- **Journey:** Personal User, Gate 2 → Gate 4 (`Calculate` → `Synthesize`)
- **Principles:** Principle 4 (`Earn the Next Step`), Principle 7 (`No Feature Without a Story`)
- **Why it matters:** This is the single highest-friction handoff in the core product. Users already did the hard part once. Making them repeat it breaks trust and kills momentum exactly at the moment they should advance to the product's highest-value output.
- **Signal:** More users who calculate a chart reach profile generation without abandoning the form.
- **Simplest version:** On profile-tab activation, pre-fill `p-*` fields from existing `c-*` values only if profile fields are blank.
- **Decision:** **Build first.** This is the highest-leverage UX fix in the current queue.

### 2. UX-006 — Post-chart CTA to AI Profile

- **Journey:** Personal User, Gate 3 → Gate 4 (`Understand` → `Synthesize`)
- **Principles:** Principle 4 (`Earn the Next Step`), Principle 9 (`One Point of Focus, One Click at a Time`)
- **Why it matters:** The chart is the opening move; the AI Profile is the differentiated value delivery. Without an explicit CTA, users are left inside a technically successful but commercially incomplete state.
- **Signal:** Higher chart-to-profile conversion rate.
- **Simplest version:** Append a single CTA card under chart results with one button to switch to `profile`.
- **Decision:** **Build second.** Pairs directly with UX-005 and should ship in the same loop if possible.

### 3. UX-007 — Home tab welcome copy split by persona

- **Journey:** Personal User, Gate 1 (`Arrive`) and Practitioner, `Activate`
- **Principles:** Principle 8 (`Practitioner Commercial Integrity`), Principle 9 (`One Point of Focus`), Principle 10 (`Honest Grounding`)
- **Why it matters:** The current home card tells the wrong story to first-time personal users. It makes the product sound like practice software before they understand the self-discovery entry point. This is positioning drift at the first-touch layer.
- **Signal:** More first-time personal users start chart generation; fewer users bounce from the overview tab.
- **Simplest version:** Branch the overview hero by tier/state: consumer-facing before chart generation for non-practitioners, practitioner-facing for practitioner or agency tiers.
- **Decision:** **Build third.** This is the first-touch correction that keeps the product coherent without undermining practitioner-first positioning.

### 4. UX-010 — Enhance tab explains why assessments improve synthesis

- **Journey:** Personal User, Gate 5 (`Deepen`)
- **Principles:** Principle 2 (`Synthesis Over Information`), Principle 3 (`Plain Language First`), Q5 (`What is the sentence that makes this matter?`)
- **Why it matters:** The assessments are strategically important because they improve synthesis specificity. Right now the product asks for effort without explaining the return. This breaks the exchange of value.
- **Signal:** Higher completion rate for behavioral validation, Big Five, and VIA flows.
- **Simplest version:** Rewrite intro/callout copy and add a success-state confirmation that says the data now feeds future AI readings.
- **Decision:** **Build fourth.** This turns a vague side quest into an intelligible enhancement loop.

### 5. UX-009 — Chart-result explanations for key terms

- **Journey:** Personal User, Gate 3 (`Understand`)
- **Principles:** Principle 2 (`Synthesis Over Information`), Principle 3 (`Plain Language First`)
- **Why it matters:** The chart currently exposes important terms without context. This weakens the user's first comprehension moment and reinforces the industry's most common complaint: "I got my chart and don't know what any of this means."
- **Signal:** More interaction with chart-result content; fewer early exits after chart render.
- **Simplest version:** Add help-icon tooltips for Soul Cross, Not-Self Theme, Split Definition, and Connection Pattern.
- **Decision:** **Build fifth.** Strong educational value, but lower leverage than the chart-to-profile path.

### 6. UX-008 — Auto-load diary, stats, achievements, leaderboard

- **Journey:** Personal User, Gate 6 (`Track`)
- **Principles:** Principle 4 (`Earn the Next Step`), Principle 9 (`One Point of Focus`)
- **Why it matters:** Manual load buttons create false-negative impressions: users think the product didn't save or doesn't work. This affects repeat-use trust more than initial activation.
- **Signal:** More diary/check-in re-engagement and fewer manual load interactions needed per session.
- **Simplest version:** Auto-load on first tab activation and refresh diary after save.
- **Decision:** **Build sixth.** Important for retention loops, but not before the activation-path fixes above.

### 7. UX-012 — Onboarding naming and intro clarity

- **Journey:** Practitioner and Personal User, orientation layer
- **Principles:** Principle 3 (`Plain Language First`), Principle 9 (`One Point of Focus`), Principle 10 (`Honest Grounding`)
- **Why it matters:** "Restart Onboarding" is the wrong label for a first-time user, and the intro currently hides the real narrative structure. This is a clarity defect, but it is not on the critical revenue path.
- **Signal:** More users start onboarding when they first encounter it.
- **Simplest version:** Rename sidebar item to `The Savannah Arc`; rewrite intro copy with explicit purpose and duration.
- **Decision:** **Build seventh.** Worth doing, but after the primary activation and comprehension fixes.

### 8. UX-011 — Check-in tooltip terminology cleanup

- **Journey:** Personal User, Gate 6 (`Track`)
- **Principles:** Principle 6 (`One Truth, One Source`), Principle 10 (`Honest Grounding`)
- **Why it matters:** This is still required for terminology consistency and trademark hygiene, but it does not materially change user progression through the core journeys.
- **Signal:** Consistent approved vocabulary in check-in surfaces.
- **Simplest version:** Replace the two tooltip strings in `index.html` with approved equivalents.
- **Decision:** **Build in the same batch as other terminology cleanup, but not ahead of higher-leverage journey items.**
