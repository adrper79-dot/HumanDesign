Pricing Sweet Spots — The Practitioner-First Revenue Architecture
THE CORE PROBLEM WITH CURRENT PRICING
The current structure has two fatal flaws:

1. Explorer at $12/mo positions the product as a commodity.
$12 says "cheap utility app." It is less than Netflix. It is less than Spotify. Users don't take cheap tools seriously, and cheap tools don't generate word of mouth. Worse — practitioners see a $12/mo app and assume their clients will use it instead of booking a reading. That makes them hostile, not evangelists.

2. Guide at $60/mo is a 10% cost on one client session.
A practitioner doing four sessions per week at $150 earns $2,400/month. Their tool budget at 3% of revenue is $72/mo. You are priced below what they'd spend on a decent coffee habit. Low price signals low quality signals low trust. And you're getting almost nothing for the most valuable customer segment you have.

3. There is no financial reason for practitioners to grow their client base on Prime Self specifically.
Right now a practitioner inviting clients to Prime Self does you a favor for free. There is no mechanism that makes them financially better off for doing so. That is the flywheel mechanism missing entirely.

THE STRATEGIC PRINCIPLE
Practitioners are not your competition. They are your sales force. Price and structure everything to make that true.

What this means in practice:

The individual tier must be positioned as a daily living tool, not a reading. "Take your practitioner's reading deeper every day" — explicitly complementary.
The AI synthesis on the individual tier should handle daily-practice questions, not replace a practitioner reading. The synthesis itself should, where appropriate, say: "For decisions of this weight, this is exactly the kind of thing to bring to a session with your practitioner."
Composite/relationship synthesis, life decision guidance, deep gate/channel work — these belong to the practitioner tier or one-time purchases, not the individual subscription.
Practitioners should actively earn money from referring their clients to Prime Self. This is the flywheel.

The Revenue Share Mechanism — The Flywheel
This is the single most important structural change.

How it works:
Every practitioner gets a unique referral link: selfprime.net/ref/[practitioner-slug]

When a client signs up through that link and upgrades to Individual ($19/mo):

Prime Self pays the practitioner 25% recurring as a credit against their own subscription bill
The credit accumulates monthly, applied automatically
No cap. No expiry.
The math:

Practitioner's active paid clients	Credit earned/month	Effective practitioner cost
0	$0	$97/mo
5	$24	$73/mo
10	$48	$49/mo
15	$71	$26/mo
20	$95	$2/mo (effectively free)
25+	$119+	$0 + surplus credit toward next month
What this does:

Practitioners are actively incentivized to encourage clients to subscribe
A practitioner with 20+ paying clients uses Prime Self essentially for free
Prime Self earns $19 × 25 = $475/mo from those clients + $97 - $119 = net $453/mo from one practitioner relationship
Practitioners talk about their credit to other practitioners → referral loops
No CAC on practitioner-sourced individual subscribers
Implementation: Tracked via the existing referral system. The referral_code on the practitioner's user record becomes their share link. The referrals table already has reward_granted and reward_value fields. Add a credit_type: 'recurring_subscription' path in the reward handler and apply it as a Stripe coupon on the practitioner's next invoice.

WHAT "NOT UNDERCUTTING PRACTITIONERS" LOOKS LIKE IN THE PRODUCT
This is not just pricing philosophy — it needs to be built into the product behavior.

1. AI response design. When a free or individual user asks something that is genuinely a practitioner-session question (major life decision, relationship dynamics, career pivot, "what should I do about X"), the AI should say: "This is a rich question that touches on [specific gates/channels]. For something this significant, I'd recommend working through it with a practitioner who can hold the full context with you. [Find a certified practitioner →]" — then still give a helpful framing, but explicitly positions the practitioner as the expert.

2. Composite gating. Full composite synthesis lives at Practitioner tier (for practitioners) and as a one-time purchase ($29) for individuals. Individual subscribers don't get unlimited composite synthesis. This keeps the "I want to understand my relationship with X" use case moving toward practitioners.

3. The practitioner directory. Every public practitioner profile is reachable from within the app whenever the AI defers to a practitioner. "Find a practitioner in your area" is a genuine feature, not just a page.

4. Client portal positioning. From the client side, the portal is branded with their practitioner's name first. "Prepared by [Practitioner Name] through Prime Self." The practitioner is front and center in every client touchpoint.
