# Practitioner-First 90-Day Roadmap

**Date:** 2026-03-14  
**Positioning:** Prime Self is practitioner-first B2B2C software. Client-facing surfaces exist primarily to help practitioners acquire, serve, and retain clients.

---

## Objective

Use the next 90 days to turn Prime Self from a feature-rich platform into a clearly positioned practitioner operating system with repeatable acquisition, activation, and retention.

This roadmap assumes:

- The flagship buyer is a solo practitioner or small agency.
- The public app, embed, directory, and API are distribution and delivery layers for that buyer.
- Agency is a premium expansion path, not the default motion.
- The biggest risks are positioning drift, workflow gaps, and business-logic inconsistency rather than lack of features.

---

## Day 90 Outcomes

By the end of this roadmap, Prime Self should be able to show all of the following:

- A clear practitioner-first homepage, pricing story, and onboarding flow.
- A flagship Practitioner plan that is easy to understand and easy to sell.
- A tighter Agency plan with documented eligibility, seat behavior, and white-label boundaries.
- A reliable core workflow: invite client -> collect birth data -> generate deliverable -> prepare session -> follow up.
- At least 10-15 active design partners using the product in real client work.
- Evidence that practitioners adopt at least two distribution surfaces: directory, embed, referrals, API, or agency seats.
- Clean tier/billing/white-label semantics across product, docs, analytics, and support language.

---

## North Star Metrics

These are the metrics that matter for the next 90 days.

- Active practitioners per week
- Practitioner activation rate: signed up -> registered -> invited first client -> generated first deliverable
- Client invite rate per practitioner
- Sessions prepared or deliverables generated per practitioner per month
- Embed installs or public directory profiles published
- Practitioner-to-agency upgrade rate
- Gross MRR by canonical tier: individual, practitioner, agency
- Net revenue retention for practitioners and agencies

Do not optimize primarily for raw consumer signups during this window.

---

## Strategic Rules

1. Every public-facing feature must answer one of three questions:
   - Does it help practitioners acquire clients?
   - Does it help practitioners deliver a better session or report?
   - Does it help practitioners retain clients or expand into a team/agency motion?
2. If a feature does none of the above, it is not a Q2 priority.
3. Agency should be sold as expansion infrastructure, not as a broader consumer subscription.
4. Internal and external tier naming must be canonical everywhere: `free`, `individual`, `practitioner`, `agency`.

---

## Phase 1: Days 1-30

### Goal

Remove positioning ambiguity and make the practitioner workflow legible from the first touchpoint.

### Product Priorities

- Rewrite homepage and upgrade messaging around practitioner outcomes, not generic self-discovery.
- Make the practitioner workflow obvious in onboarding:
  - Register as practitioner
  - Create public profile
  - Invite first client
  - Generate first report
- Tighten the practitioner dashboard around three primary actions:
  - Add/invite client
  - Generate or review deliverables
  - Prepare next session
- Align client-facing touchpoints with practitioner ownership:
  - public directory profile
  - embed widget
  - branded PDF/report outputs
- Resolve tier naming, billing, and white-label inconsistencies documented in the companion audit.

### Pricing Priorities

- Make Practitioner the flagship plan.
- Treat Individual as a support tier, not the product story.
- Keep Agency published only if the package is precise and supportable.
- If Agency scope is still fluid, convert the CTA from instant checkout to contact/demo qualification.
- Publish explicit seat, white-label, API, and webhook limits for Agency.

### GTM Priorities

- Recruit 10-15 design partners from target practitioner segments:
  - Human design readers
  - astrology-adjacent practitioners
  - coaches and facilitators already selling sessions
- Run concierge onboarding for every design partner.
- Capture baseline proof from each partner:
  - current workflow
  - time spent per client
  - report prep pain points
  - pricing model
- Produce 3-5 short case studies from real usage, not aspirational copy.

### Deliverables by Day 30

- Practitioner-first homepage brief and copy
- Simplified pricing brief
- Design partner program doc and outreach list
- Activation dashboard for practitioner signup -> first client -> first deliverable
- Resolved tier naming and entitlement drift on the critical path

---

## Phase 2: Days 31-60

### Goal

Deepen workflow value so practitioners have a reason to stay, not just a reason to try.

### Product Priorities

- Turn session preparation into a first-class workflow:
  - client summary
  - previous notes
  - recommended focus areas
  - ready-to-use talking points
- Improve follow-up and retention surfaces:
  - branded post-session summaries
  - client reminders
  - next-session prompts
- Strengthen directory and embed as acquisition surfaces:
  - practitioner bios
  - booking links
  - clear call to action from embed to practitioner-owned next step
- Make agency seats and permissions understandable:
  - owner vs seat member capabilities
  - inherited practitioner access
  - limits and upgrade paths

### Pricing Priorities

- Introduce annual Practitioner pricing if retention signals are healthy.
- Preserve beta pricing only for design partners with explicit end dates.
- Lock Agency packaging to a small number of concrete promises:
  - seat count
  - white-label rules
  - API access
  - custom webhooks or integrations

### GTM Priorities

- Launch practitioner-specific content, not general spiritual app marketing:
  - how to use Prime Self in client sessions
  - how to embed chart capture on a practitioner site
  - how to prepare faster and deliver better reports
- Run live demos for practitioners and agencies.
- Build partner loops:
  - referral incentives for practitioners
  - cross-promotion with educators or communities
  - onboarding templates for agency owners

### Deliverables by Day 60

- Session prep workflow live
- Better follow-up/reporting workflow live
- Directory and embed onboarding cleaned up
- Practitioner case studies published
- Annual and beta pricing decision memo

---

## Phase 3: Days 61-90

### Goal

Prove repeatability and make Agency expansion operationally safe.

### Product Priorities

- Polish white-label and expansion infrastructure:
  - agency seats
  - API key management
  - custom webhook clarity
  - branded distribution surfaces
- Improve admin and business visibility:
  - correct MRR reporting by canonical tier
  - tier distribution
  - agency seat adoption
  - embed adoption
- Add reliability and trust layers around billing and entitlement handling.

### Pricing Priorities

- If Agency closes cleanly and support burden is manageable, keep self-serve checkout.
- If support burden is high, move Agency to contact sales with a qualification flow.
- Set price only after validating actual agency demand for seats, white-label, and API.

### GTM Priorities

- Build a repeatable sales motion for Practitioner and Agency:
  - outreach script
  - demo flow
  - objection handling
  - onboarding checklist
- Publish vertical landing pages for clear practitioner segments.
- Add proof assets to every core conversion path.

### Deliverables by Day 90

- Canonical practitioner positioning live across site and pricing
- Reliable canonical revenue/tier reporting
- Agency scope clearly documented and supportable
- 10-15 live practitioner references or active design partners
- Documented GTM playbook for practitioner acquisition and activation

---

## Product Decisions To Make Now

These decisions unblock execution and should not drift beyond the first 2 weeks.

1. Is Agency self-serve or sales-assisted?
2. Is hide-attribution a Practitioner benefit or Agency-only benefit?
3. Is Individual strategically important, or only a support/upsell tier?
4. Which client-facing artifacts are truly practitioner-owned and branded?
5. What exact action defines practitioner activation in product analytics?

---

## Not In Scope For This 90-Day Window

- Broad consumer growth campaigns
- New spirituality categories or speculative modalities
- Large marketplace expansion before the practitioner workflow is sharp
- Major new feature families unrelated to practitioner acquisition, delivery, or retention

---

## Recommended Execution Order

1. Fix canonical tier, billing, and white-label drift.
2. Reposition homepage, pricing, and onboarding around practitioners.
3. Prove activation with design partners.
4. Deepen session prep and follow-up workflows.
5. Expand Agency only after the Practitioner motion is reliable.
